Donate for Flood at Noakhali, Bangladesh

In 2024, Noakhali experienced severe flooding that has had a devastating impact on the region. Starting in August, heavy rainfall caused waterlogging in the district, which worsened throughout August and September. Over 2 million people were affected, with homes, roads, and marketplaces submerged in waist-deep water. The floods were exacerbated by clogged canals and wetlands, as illegal encroachments blocked the natural drainage of water.Eight upazilas, including Begumganj, Sadar, and Senbagh, have been hit the hardest. Many families were forced to seek shelter in schools and other public institutions, which disrupted daily life and education.

Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

The Quota Reform Movement in Bangladesh, which started in 2018, saw numerous protests demanding reforms in the government job quota system. During these demonstrations, several participants were injured in clashes with law enforcement agencies. In response to the injuries sustained by students and activists, some organizations and individuals provided aid. The injured received medical treatment, and in many cases, financial support was extended to cover their healthcare costs. Civil society groups, student bodies, and human rights organizations also expressed solidarity with the injured protesters and demanded justice. Human rights organizations like Ain o Salish Kendra (ASK) and Bangladesh Legal Aid and Services Trust (BLAST) called for investigations into police actions and the excessive use of force.
